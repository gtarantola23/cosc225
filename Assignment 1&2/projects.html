Swimming Time Predictor

My goal is to use the historical times of a specific swimmer to predict their future performances. Machine learning can help achieve this goal by analyzing a swimmer's performance over time to determine whether they are dropping time or adding time and using these patterns to predict future performance. So, Machine learning algorithms can help identify a relationship between past times and future improvements based on time series data. I obtained my data from USAswimming.com, and got historical statistics of two prominent swimmers. This project looks at various regression models, and compares them in regards to accuracy and time. Regresssion models will allow me to have a training and testing set while predicting a continuous variable. It will be able to look at my data and see if there is a relationship. If linear regression does not work very well, I will try random forest regression because the averaging of many decision trees may cause more accurate predictions by reducing the variation in results. Generally, I would look for a mean square error that corresponds to 3 seconds, some math will be needed to calculate what is equivalent to three seconds after standardization. This cutoff was determined by the relative amount of time this corresponds to in swimming. A 3-second difference in any swimming event is a considerable difference in time/placement. That said, I believe it is a reasonable cutoff, as predicting a time within 3 seconds is a generally good estimate.
